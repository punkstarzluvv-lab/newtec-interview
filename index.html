00 · Why this role is different

This Is Not a Social Media Job

Insurance technology marketing is not normal social media. The goal is not to post more content and wait for organic growth. The goal is to translate technical software value into clear buyer pain, route that pain-aware audience through a focused landing page, and convert attention into qualified demo conversations.

The buyers are MGAs, carriers, and underwriting operators — people making operational decisions about software platforms. They don't respond to hype. They respond to content that names their exact problem, landing pages that make the next step obvious, and follow-up that doesn't feel like a mass email.

If brought into this role, I would build that system — not just manage a posting calendar.

The main KPI is not likes. The main KPI is qualified demo conversations.

03 · The buyers I would focus on first

Two ICPs. Not Five.

Most B2B campaigns fail because they try to reach everyone. My starting focus would be two decision-makers most aligned with NewTec's public positioning around carriers and MGAs. Two sharp personas outperform five shallow ones.

MGA Founder / Program Administrator

Core Pain

Launching new programs takes too long. Spreadsheet-heavy workflows don't scale. Carrier relationships require clean reporting and professional systems — and they need them fast before competitors catch up.

Content Hook

"What would it cost to launch your next program 60 days faster?"

Demo Trigger

Speed, credibility, and operational control. They want to know they can scale without hiring more staff or rebuilding their process every year.

Best Channel

LinkedIn thought leadership and targeted outreach. They're active, they're looking for edge, and they respond to content that names specific pain — not buzzwords about digital transformation.

Carrier Operations Leader / VP of Underwriting

Core Pain

Data comes from too many sources. Reporting is inconsistent. Compliance documentation is manual. Integration with existing systems is slow and expensive — and the team feels it every Friday afternoon.

Content Hook

"What if your underwriting team got their Friday afternoon back?"

Demo Trigger

Cleaner data pipelines, automated reporting, better workflow visibility — without losing the control they've built into their current process.

Best Channel

LinkedIn and industry newsletters. They read more than they post. Educational content that names their exact operational headache — not product feature lists — gets them to click.

LinkedIn Content

I would publish content targeting specific insurance-tech pain — bordereaux, manual endorsements, slow program launches, reporting gaps. Not generic "digital transformation" language. Each post ends with a clear, low-friction next step routing pain-aware buyers to a landing page. The goal is not impressions. The goal is qualified clicks from people who recognize their own problem in the post.

Goal → qualified clicks, not impressions

Pain-Aware Landing Page

One page. One specific buyer pain. One CTA. No navigation. No distraction. The headline mirrors the content that brought them here — if the post was about spreadsheet-heavy MGA workflows, the landing page opens with: "Still managing your MGA operations on spreadsheets?" — not a product overview. Continuity between content and landing page is what prevents drop-off at this stage.

Goal → demo page visits from the right people

Demo CTA

Not "Learn More." Not "Contact Us." The ask is specific, low-friction, and outcome-focused: "Book a 20-minute platform walkthrough." That phrasing removes pressure. It signals a conversation, not a sales pitch. I would test CTA wording, placement, and button copy to find what converts best for NewTec's specific buyers and where they are in their decision journey.

Goal → demo form opens from interested buyers

Lead Capture

Short enough to not lose people. Specific enough to qualify intent. Fields: name, company, role, email, and one open question — "What's your biggest operational challenge right now?" That single field pre-qualifies intent, surfaces objections before the demo, and gives the sales conversation a real head start before the call even happens.

Goal → form submissions with role + context captured

Email Follow-Up

Immediately after submission: confirmation plus one useful piece of content tied to what they said their challenge was. 48 hours later, a short specific follow-up — not a generic drip sequence. Subject: "Quick follow-up on your demo request." Preview: "One thing most MGAs notice in the first walkthrough…" I would build and monitor this workflow so leads keep moving, not sitting in a queue.

Goal → reply rate, open rate, demo held

Booked Demo

This is the only output that actually counts. Not follower count. Not form submissions sitting unread. A qualified insurance professional on a live call with the NewTec team — who showed up because content named their exact pain, a landing page made the next step obvious, and a follow-up kept them engaged long enough to commit. That is what I would build toward every week, and what I would measure every week.

Main KPI → booked demo conversations with qualified buyers

06 · First 30 days if brought into this role

A Starting Framework, Not a Guarantee

Week one is about understanding before anything goes live. The plan below reflects how I would structure the first month — subject to what I find in the baseline audit on day one.

Baseline + Audit

  • Audit current website, social channels, landing pages, demo CTA, lead forms, and email follow-up workflows
  • Establish baseline metrics: traffic, LinkedIn engagement, lead volume, current demo booking rate
  • Map the existing content-to-demo path and identify the biggest drop-off point
  • Work with leadership to clarify ICP priorities — who is the most important buyer right now and what pain are they most responsive to?
  • Set week-four targets based on real baselines, not assumptions made before day one

Buyer Messaging + Pain-Based Content

  • Build a content calendar around specific insurance-tech pain points: bordereaux, endorsements, manual reporting, program launch speed, and compliance gaps
  • Write 4–6 LinkedIn posts targeting MGA founders and carrier operations leaders — each anchored to real operational pain with a clear landing page CTA
  • Draft one focused landing page headline, subhead, and CTA for the primary buyer identified in week one
  • Set up or improve the email follow-up sequence for demo form submissions
  • Begin publishing and track what gets engagement versus what gets actual clicks toward the demo page

Demo Campaign Launch

  • Launch one focused landing page targeting the primary ICP with one specific campaign angle
  • Run the LinkedIn content campaign and route engaged visitors directly to the landing page
  • Test the full funnel end-to-end: content → landing page → form → email follow-up → demo booking
  • Monitor demo page visits, form submissions, and email open rates daily
  • Identify one element that is not working and cut it before week four rather than letting it drag

Review + Improve

  • Review all KPIs: clicks, demo page visits, form submissions, lead quality, follow-up response rate, and booked demos held
  • Compare results against the week-one baseline — where did the system work and where did it break down?
  • Cut what did not create movement toward demos. Double down on what did.
  • Present findings and a clear month-two recommendation to leadership
  • Week four determines the roadmap — not assumptions made before the first piece of content went live
